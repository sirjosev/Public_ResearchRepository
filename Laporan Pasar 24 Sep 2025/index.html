Gold Hits New All-Time High

Gold prices surged to a new record, breaking US$3,800 per troy ounce. This rally is fueled by expectations of further interest rate cuts by the U.S. Federal Reserve and heightened geopolitical uncertainty, which increases demand for safe-haven assets. Projections suggest prices could even reach US$4,000 to US$5,000 next year, creating a significant tailwind for gold-related equities.

Key Drivers of the Rally

1

Fed Rate Cut Expectations

Market probability for Fed rate cuts in October and December stands at 89.8% and 75.4% respectively, making non-yielding gold more attractive.

↓
2

Geopolitical Instability

Ongoing global tensions and political uncertainty in major economies enhance gold's role as a primary safe-haven asset for investors.

3

Central Bank Purchases

Aggressive buying from central banks, particularly from China, continues to absorb supply and provide strong support for higher prices.

Riding the Golden Wave: Sector Performance

The gold price surge has directly translated into strong performance for Indonesian gold mining and producing equities.

Gold Stock Daily Gains (%)

Leading gold-related stocks on the IDX posted significant gains, outperforming the broader market as investors capitalized on the rising commodity price. ARCI led the pack with an impressive 7.34% increase.

Foreign Net Buy on Gold Stocks

Foreign investors showed strong interest, accumulating positions in key gold stocks. ANTM was the clear favorite, attracting over 70% of the foreign inflow among this peer group.
